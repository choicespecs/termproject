A. High Fidelity Prototypes

Feedback for Students

Previously, we have shown how results can affect and help students throughout their learning process. Our group has worked towards creating an interface that can assist students in gathering this information especially in hybrid online synchronous classrooms.

Feedback for Instructors

Feedback for instructors is crucial is maintaining a balanced "pace" in their classroom. This will bridge the gap for instructors to help maintain this "pace" for all students. This interface will help students non-intrusively give feedback to teachers in online synchronous classrooms.

Levels of Complexity

Addressing levels of complexity can address the issues with current solutions involving addressing both feedback and engagement. Our prototype will work on creating an interface that can help give instructors more options in terms of addressing their personal needs.

B. Testing Protocol

1. Testing Protocol

Our team’s mission is to develop an efficient solution for pedagogical issues that face both teachers and students. The solution we offer is an application that affords to not only serve as a platform for communication between students and teachers, but also gives students and teachers plenty of tools and feedback to increase success on both ends. Through integration of layered functions, our goal is to offer the best learning experience for students, teachers and business professionals. We aim to improve classroom focus, increase learning retention and enhance virtual classrooms. Given our design, the proposed research question is as follows: How can we measure knowledge retention and overall immersion of a TeamWorkWork user?

In order for us to correctly answer the proposed research question there are several factors to consider. One factor to consider are the stakeholders involved. Our design aims to satisfy the needs of students, faculty and business professionals. By defining the end user, we can directly adjust various metrics or features accordingly. Another factor to consider are the methodologies our team aims to include for testing. Given the context of our question as it applies to the end users we can create several testing methods for evaluation. Our team decided on observation/participant observation, surveys, experiments and secondary data analysis for testing methodologies to help answer our proposed research question.

2. Testing Procedure

Before implementing a testing procedure it is imperative to gain informed consent from the experimental groups. We have several ways of achieving informed consent with our stakeholders such as feedback request emails, surveys such as the one we provided in milestone 2, and pop up notifications built into the application which indicates to users that feedback and diagnostics will be collected unless they wish to opt out. Our survey asks several questions such as: “How much did you enjoy using TeamWorkWork?”, “Would you say that TeamWorkWork improved your engagement with your audience/presenter?”, “How likely are you to recommend TeamWorkWork to your peers?”, and “In what ways could TeamWorkWork improve your experience with the app (optional)?” Providing a numerical scale for qualitative questions like those in the survey, allows us to numericise user responses and effectively answer our proposed research question. We know that users who score high on enjoyment and answer yes to improved engagement prove the success of our design.

Collecting feedback and diagnostics for our software, unless users choose to opt out, allows us to extrapolate and expand our design further. Data such as average time spent on the application, most used functions, and answer accuracy are vital for quality control. This data is also important to our proposed research question from a statistical perspective. We can gauge immersiveness through average time spent on the program, and measure knowledge retention through gradebooks which are also available to teachers. Teachers can also utilize the gradebook as a means of developing curriculum or compare it with in person scores. As many of the solutions we provide currently exist in various educational websites or softwares, secondary data analysis may provide benefit to the design. Applying the same research question to websites such as kahoot then analyzing available data from kahoots API such as quiz accuracy or quiz participation will allow us to develop a more enriching educational tool. Since our testing procedures will be conducted completely online, it will comply with social distancing guidelines and be safe enough even in times like a pandemic.
